MathJax Basics

MathJax is enabled across all pages of this website. This allows you to include mathematical equations and expressions in your content using LaTeX notation. Here's how to use it:

  • For inline equations, use $...$ or \(...\)
  • For display equations, use $$...$$ or \[...\]
  • Standard LaTeX math commands are supported
  • For multi-line equations, use LaTeX environments like aligned, gather, etc.

Inline Math

Inline equations are embedded directly within text. For example, typing $E=mc^2$ will render as $E=mc^2$.

Examples:

  • The Pythagorean theorem: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$
  • The area of a circle: $A = \pi r^2$
  • The derivative of a function: $\frac{df}{dx} = \lim_{h \to 0}\frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$
  • Euler's identity: $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$

Display Math

Display equations stand alone in their own block and are centered. Use them for more complex or important equations that deserve their own space.

Example of a display equation (Maxwell's first equation):

$$\nabla \cdot \vec{E} = \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}$$

Multi-line aligned equations:

$$\begin{aligned} (a+b)^2 &= (a+b)(a+b)\\ &= a(a+b) + b(a+b)\\ &= a^2 + ab + ba + b^2\\ &= a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \end{aligned}$$

Advanced Features

Matrices

$$\begin{pmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{pmatrix}$$

Fractions and Binomials

$$\frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} = \binom{n}{k}$$

Integrals and Summations

$$\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i) \, \Delta x$$

Greek Letters

Lowercase: $\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta, \epsilon, \zeta, \eta, \theta, \ldots$

Uppercase: $\Gamma, \Delta, \Theta, \Lambda, \Xi, \Pi, \Sigma, \Phi, \Psi, \Omega$
